## Summary

Software developers planning to attend CES 2024 have a lot to look forward to, with a lineup of engaging talks and events. Expect insightful discussions from employees at leading tech companies like Google, Facebook, and Microsoft, focusing on the latest advancements in AI and machine learning. Additionally, there will be panels exploring the future of smart homes and the Internet of Things – offering something of interest for every attendee. This is a golden opportunity to gain knowledge from industry experts and network with peers in the field. Keep an eye out for more updates and exciting announcements as the event draws closer.

## Details

- Pre-show: Sunday, January 7th, Monday, January 8th
- Starts: Tuesday, January 9th
- Ends: Friday, January 12th
- Location: Las Vegas, NV
- Website: https://www.ces.tech/schedule.aspx

## Pre-Show

The excitement at CES 2024 begins on Sunday, January 7th, with Media Days. The event starts with ShowStoppers Launchit, a platform where ten innovative startups will present their products to journalists and potential investors. This is followed by CES Unveiled, offering further opportunities for emerging companies to connect with the media. CES actively works with media outlets and influencers to promote the event and provide the latest industry news and trends.

Monday, another Media Day, will see LG Electronics sharing insights into their latest technologies and discussing their evolution into a Smart Life Solution Company. This transformation focuses on leveraging AI to enhance living spaces and customer experiences. Additionally, Ambient Scientific is set to reveal an ultra-low power AI processor designed for Edge and IoT devices, capable of running on-device AI applications with minimal power usage. The day will also include discussions on "The AI Inflection Point – Entertainment, Internet & Media," among other topics related to AI integration. The day's highlight will be a keynote by Siemens' Roland Busch, discussing the transformative impact of AI and the Industrial Metaverse on our daily lives.

## Main Event

### Tuesday, January 9th

The official opening occurs on Tuesday morning, leading into presentations including:

- "Augmented & Virtual Reality in the Creator Ecosystem" presented by Lianne Baron from Meta. This session will delve into the impact and integration of AR and VR technologies within the creative community.
- "What Companies Need to Know about How People Understand Privacy" featuring David Evans, Ph.D. in Social Psychology from Microsoft. This talk will explore the complex dynamics of privacy perception, questioning whether companies are offering true privacy or merely an illusion of it.
- "How Legacy Tech is Talking to Creators," led by entertainment professional Max Brabant from Meta, will shed light on how major tech companies are fostering unique voices on their platforms. The discussion will focus on new products, platforms, and marketing strategies designed to create spaces for diverse creators.
- "How Brands Are Embracing Content Creators as Marketing Partners" by Santi Pochat from Microsoft LinkedIn. This session aims to illustrate how consumer and tech brands have integrated creators into their brand storytelling strategies, leveraging authentic voices to deepen brand connection.
- In "Accelerating Developer Experience: Unleash Your Full Potential," Ovetta Sampson, Director of UX Machine Learning at Google, will discuss the importance of enhancing the developer experience to spur product innovation and operational efficiency.
- "AI is the 5th Industrial Revolution" presented by Dimitri Kusnezov, Under Secretary for Science and Technology at DHS. This talk offers insights into how data-driven strategies can revolutionize brand operations, scaling, and efficiency for improved profitability, output, and customer satisfaction.
- "The Creators Who Are Leaning Into AI" will explore the evolving roles of users and creators in the age of GenAI, examining how this technology opens new avenues for personal and artistic expression.

### Wednesday, January 10th

Doors reopen on Wednesday for another full day of presenting consumer electronics.

- "How Market Demand for VR/AR Headsets will Impact the Metaverse and Spatial Computing," featuring Young Lee from Meta. This session will delve into the significant role VR/AR headsets play in shaping and expanding the market for a virtual computing future.
- Mark Grether, Vice President and General Manager of Uber Advertising at Uber, presents "Uber: From Pickup to Purchase - Navigating the New Road of Urban Mobility and Innovation." This talk will provide insights into the evolving urban mobility landscape, its impact on consumer behavior, and how these changes are opening new avenues for business transformation and advertising opportunities.
- "Headliner Conversation with Steve Huffman, Co-Founder and CEO, Reddit" will feature Steve Huffman discussing the latest developments and future prospects at Reddit.
- In "The Tipping Point for AI," Ovetta Sampson, Google's Director of UX Machine Learning, returns to explore how the entertainment and media industry can harness AI to unlock new possibilities and foster significant growth.

### Thursday, January 11th

The party is just getting started as a new stable of speakers enter stage right on Thursday:
Christine Trodella of Meta's Reality Labs takes part in "The New Workplace Toolbox", discussing the latest tools and emerging technologies being used to get ahead.

- Christine Trodella from Meta's Reality Labs will participate in "The New Workplace Toolbox." This session will discuss the latest tools and emerging technologies that are shaping the future of work.
- Alexander Dranov takes the startup stage with "Enhancing Developers' Experience with Siemens and Microsoft's AI." This presentation will highlight how the Siemens Xcelerator Developer Portal, developed in partnership with Microsoft, is leveraging inner source and industry standards to improve workflow efficiency and introduce a generative AI assistant.
- "AI 2024" is a panel discussion featuring representatives from Google and Microsoft, including Google's Addie Cooke and Microsoft's Danyelle Solomon. They will explore global competition in AI technology, investment, and research and development. Key topics include the nature of countries' competition over AI regulatory policy and the components of a successful strategy in this arena.
- Simone Hall Wood, Meta's Privacy and Public Policy Manager, will contribute to "The U.S. Privacy Landscape - Consumers Deserve Better Than a Patchwork of Confusion." This talk will address the current state of U.S. consumer privacy regulations and the need for a more coherent approach.

## Conclusion

CES 2024 is set to wrap up on Friday, with the day featuring a range of talks, including the all-day "Shark Tank Open Call," where leading entrepreneurs will present their most innovative business ideas and products.

As CES 2024 nears its end, attendees can look forward to assimilating the wealth of knowledge and insights the event promises to offer. With an array of talks and panels covering crucial topics like AI and machine learning, smart homes, and the Internet of Things, software developers are in an excellent position to stay current with the latest industry trends and technologies. In our fast-evolving industry, staying informed and engaged is key, and CES is the perfect venue for this pursuit.
